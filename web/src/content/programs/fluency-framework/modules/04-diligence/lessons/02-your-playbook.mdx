## The capstone

You've done the reading. You've written the homework. Now assemble
it into the single artifact that makes this course worth it: a
one-page personal playbook you use every time you work with AI.

## The template

A working playbook has six sections. Steal this as-is, then make it
yours:

### 1. Who I am (3 sentences)

From Module 2, Lesson 3. The sentence that tells an AI who's asking.

### 2. What I don't delegate

From Module 1, Lesson 2. Your 5 items + short reasons.

### 3. My delegation rubric

From Module 1, Lesson 3. Adapt the stakes/reversibility/ability
table to your actual life. Keep it small.

### 4. My description template

From Module 2, Lessons 1–2. Your reusable 4-part shape:
goal · constraints · non-constraints · evidence of success.

### 5. My discernment checklist

From Module 3. Your 3 checks, in the order you run them, with any
domain-specific additions (e.g. for a nonprofit operator: "fact-
check any dollar figure," "verify any funder name").

### 6. My disclosure line

From Module 4, Lesson 1. The exact sentence you'll put at the bottom
of AI-assisted work.

## How to use it

Keep the playbook one click away — a pinned note, a Notion page, a
printout on your wall. Read it before you start an AI-assisted task.
Revise it monthly for the first six months. Your playbook should
*change* as you learn.

## Share it (optional)

If you want — and only if you want — post your playbook in the
lesson discussion below. Everyone here can read and learn from each
other's playbooks. Copying someone else's playbook word-for-word is
*not* fluency; writing your own is.

## What's next

You've finished Framework & Foundations. The other tracks in the
School of AI Fluency — Students, Educators, Nonprofits, Teaching —
go deeper on contexts this one touched only briefly. Pick the one
that fits your life and keep going.

Or jump into another school entirely. Devin School teaches you to
ship PRs. Claude School goes deep on reading, writing, and
reasoning. Gemini School is for researchers. You're fluent now;
every class in every school should go faster from here.
